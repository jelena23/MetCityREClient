Stari Grad

The Municipality of Stari Grad is the city municipality of the City of Belgrade. It occupies an area of 698 ha, with a population of 48,450 inhabitants.

The name of the Old Town reflects the historical place and role of the old city center from where Belgrade further developed. The present municipality was created and adopted today's name in 1957 by merging the municipalities of Stari grad, Skadarlija and the part of Terazije.

The day of the municipality is the glory of Cveta, which is celebrated last week before Easter.

Savski Venac

Municipality of Savski venac is the city municipality of the City of Belgrade. It occupies an area of ​​15.8 km². It has 39,122 inhabitants, while twice more people work in it [1]. Savski venac is the traffic, tourist and business center of Belgrade, and it is also one of the oldest municipalities [1]. It is located at the mouth of the Topcider River in Sava at Topčider Hill and its slopes.

The name of the municipality of Savski Venac symbolizes its position. It is named because it is placed like a wreath along the very shore of the Sava. After the expansion of Belgrade outside the Kalemegdan wall, new settlements developed on the banks of the Danube towards Dorćol and on the coast of Sava to Savamala. By the arrival of the Serbian Prince Miloš Obrenović in Topčider and the construction of konaki, at the beginning of the 19th century, the Savski venac became the center of the political and public life of the city [1].

Vracar

City municipality Vračar is a Belgrade municipality. Surface this is the smallest and densely populated Belgrade municipality: it occupies an area of 292 - {ha} - with about 60,000 inhabitants.

There are several legends about becoming a Vracar. The oldest dates from 1521, when this place was named after the hero-unbeliever whose name was Vracar, who had a hut there. On the other, the name derives from sparrow fields, because many of these birds were settled in the territory of today's Vracar.

The glory of the Vracar Municipality is Sveti Sava, and it is celebrated on January 27th.
